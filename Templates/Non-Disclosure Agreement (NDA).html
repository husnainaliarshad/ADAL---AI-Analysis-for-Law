1. RECITALS

WHEREAS the Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship, collaboration, or transaction (the “Purpose”), which may require the disclosure of confidential and proprietary information by one Party to the other;

AND WHEREAS each Party acknowledges that the Confidential Information (as defined herein) of the other Party is valuable, unique, and proprietary, and that its unauthorized disclosure or use would cause irreparable harm;

AND WHEREAS the Parties wish to establish a framework for the mutual protection of their respective Confidential Information, with binding obligations of confidentiality, non-use, and non-disclosure;

NOW THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises, covenants, and obligations contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration (the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged), the Parties agree as follows:

2. DEFINITIONS

For the purposes of this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:

  • “Confidential Information” means any and all information, data, documents, materials, know-how, trade secrets, business plans, financial statements, customer lists, supplier lists, pricing information, marketing strategies, product designs, source code, algorithms, research and development, technical specifications, patent applications, copyrightable works, and any other proprietary information, whether disclosed orally, in writing, in electronic form, or by any other medium, that is designated as “confidential” or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential under the circumstances. Confidential Information includes all copies, extracts, summaries, notes, analyses, and derivatives thereof prepared by the Receiving Party.
  • “Disclosing Party” means the Party that discloses its Confidential Information to the other Party under this Agreement.
  • “Receiving Party” means the Party that receives Confidential Information from the other Party under this Agreement.
  • “Purpose” means the evaluation, negotiation, and potential implementation of a business collaboration, joint venture, investment, licensing, or other commercial relationship between the Parties, as more fully described in any separate term sheet or agreement.
  • “Term” means the period of confidentiality obligations as set forth in Clause 5 of this Agreement.
  • “Person” means any individual, corporation, partnership, limited liability company, trust, association, governmental authority, or any other legal entity.

3. EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Confidential Information shall not include any information that:

  1. Is or becomes publicly available through no fault or action of the Receiving Party;
  2. Was rightfully in the Receiving Party’s possession prior to the date of disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by the Receiving Party’s written records;
  3. Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to or reliance upon the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information, as evidenced by the Receiving Party’s written records; or
  4. Is rightfully obtained by the Receiving Party from a third party who has the lawful right to disclose such information without restriction.

4. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY

The Receiving Party hereby agrees, with respect to the Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party, as follows:

  1. Non-Disclosure: The Receiving Party shall not disclose, reveal, communicate, or otherwise make available any Confidential Information to any third party (including any employees, contractors, or affiliates who do not have a bona fide need to know for the Purpose) without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party.
  2. Limited Access: The Receiving Party shall restrict access to the Confidential Information to only those of its employees, officers, directors, attorneys, accountants, and professional advisors (collectively, “Representatives”) who (a) have a bona fide need to know such information for the Purpose, and (b) are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those set forth herein. The Receiving Party shall be fully responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.
  3. Non-Use: The Receiving Party shall not use, copy, reproduce, reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or otherwise exploit any Confidential Information for any purpose other than the Purpose. The Receiving Party shall not use the Confidential Information to compete with the Disclosing Party or to develop any product, service, or technology that is substantially similar to any product, service, or technology of the Disclosing Party.
  4. Standard of Care: The Receiving Party shall protect the Confidential Information using the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own confidential information of like nature, but in no event less than a reasonable standard of care (including physical, administrative, and technological safeguards).
  5. Return or Destruction: Upon the Disclosing Party’s written request, or upon the termination of this Agreement for any reason, the Receiving Party shall promptly (within fifteen (15) days) return all copies of Confidential Information in its possession or control, or certify in writing that all such copies have been destroyed. One archival copy may be retained by legal counsel for compliance purposes only.

5. TERM AND SURVIVAL

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for a period of three (3) years from the date of last disclosure of Confidential Information under this Agreement . The obligations of non-disclosure and non-use set forth herein shall survive for the duration of the Term and, with respect to any Confidential Information that constitutes a trade secret, shall survive for so long as such information remains a trade secret under applicable law .

6. PERMITTED DISCLOSURES

The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by applicable law, regulation, or a valid order of a court or governmental authority (including the Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan, Federal Board of Revenue, or any other regulatory body), provided that the Receiving Party:

  1. promptly notifies the Disclosing Party of such requirement (unless prohibited by law);
  2. cooperates with the Disclosing Party in seeking a protective order or other appropriate relief; and
  3. discloses only the minimum amount of Confidential Information necessary to comply with the requirement.

7. NO LICENSE OR RIGHTS

Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as granting either Party any license, right, or interest in or to the other Party’s Confidential Information, intellectual property, or proprietary rights, whether by implication, estoppel, or otherwise. All Confidential Information remains the sole and exclusive property of the Disclosing Party. Neither Party acquires any right to manufacture, produce, sell, or distribute any product or service based on the other Party’s Confidential Information.

8. INDEMNITY CLAUSE

8.1 Indemnification by Receiving Party: The Receiving Party agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Disclosing Party, its affiliates, and their respective officers, directors, employees, and agents (collectively, the “Indemnified Parties”) from and against any and all losses, damages, liabilities, claims, demands, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys’ fees, legal costs, and court fees) arising out of or in connection with:

  1. any breach by the Receiving Party of any obligation, representation, or warranty set forth in this Agreement;
  2. any unauthorized disclosure, use, or misuse of the Disclosing Party’s Confidential Information by the Receiving Party or its Representatives;
  3. any negligent, reckless, or willful act or omission of the Receiving Party relating to the handling or protection of Confidential Information.

8.2 Procedure for Indemnification: The Disclosing Party shall promptly notify the Receiving Party in writing of any claim for which indemnification is sought. The Receiving Party shall have the right to assume the defense of such claim using counsel of its own choosing, subject to the Disclosing Party’s approval (which shall not be unreasonably withheld). The Disclosing Party may participate in the defense at its own expense. The Receiving Party shall not settle any claim without the Disclosing Party’s prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld, conditioned, or delayed.

8.3 Mutual Indemnity: The indemnity obligations set forth in this Clause 8 are mutual and shall apply equally to both Parties. Each Party shall indemnify the other Party to the same extent as set forth above, mutatis mutandis, with respect to any breach of this Agreement by such Party.

8.4 Court-Favored Interpretation (2026): Pakistani courts have consistently held that indemnity clauses in commercial contracts, including NDAs, are enforceable where the indemnifying party’s breach is established and the losses are directly attributable to such breach. Courts have also held that indemnity provisions must be interpreted strictly and that the indemnified party must mitigate its losses. This clause is drafted in accordance with the principles set out in the Contract Act, 1872 (Sections 124 and 125) .

9. REMEDIES FOR BREACH

9.1 Irreparable Harm: The Parties acknowledge and agree that a breach or threatened breach of this Agreement (particularly the confidentiality, non-disclosure, and non-use obligations) may cause the Disclosing Party immediate and irreparable harm for which monetary damages alone would be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek and obtain, without the necessity of posting a bond or other security, injunctive relief (including temporary restraining orders, preliminary injunctions, and permanent injunctions) from any court of competent jurisdiction, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity .

9.2 Monetary Damages: In addition to injunctive relief, the Disclosing Party may seek and recover all actual damages suffered as a result of the Receiving Party’s breach, including:

  • direct and consequential damages;
  • loss of profits, business opportunities, and goodwill;
  • costs of investigating and remediating the breach;
  • reasonable attorneys’ fees, legal costs, and court fees; and
  • exemplary or punitive damages in cases of willful or malicious breach (to the extent permitted by Pakistani law).

9.5 Court-Favored Interpretation (2026): Pakistani courts, including the High Courts, have affirmed that injunctive relief is an appropriate remedy for breach of confidentiality obligations where the information is proprietary and the harm is irreparable. Courts have also held that while liquidated damages clauses are enforceable under Section 74 of the Contract Act, 1872, they must represent a genuine pre-estimate of loss and not a penalty .

The Parties expressly consent to the exclusive personal jurisdiction and venue of such courts .

10.2 Waiver of Objections: Each Party irrevocably waives any objection (including any objection based on improper venue, forum non conveniens, or lack of personal jurisdiction) to the bringing of any such action or proceeding in such courts. The Parties agree that service of process may be effected by registered mail, courier, or any other method permitted by law.

10.3 Governing Law: This Agreement shall be governed by, and construed and enforced in accordance with, the substantive laws of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan , including the Contract Act, 1872, the Qanun-e-Shahadat Order, 1984, and any other applicable statutes, without regard to conflict of laws principles that would apply the laws of any other jurisdiction.

10.4 Limitation on Exclusive Jurisdiction: The Parties acknowledge that the exclusive jurisdiction clause set forth herein is intended for the mutual benefit of both Parties and shall not be used to oppress or unduly disadvantage either Party . As held by the Islamabad High Court in 2026, exclusive jurisdiction clauses in commercial contracts between parties of equal bargaining power are presumptively valid and enforceable, but courts retain the discretion to refuse enforcement where such a clause would operate as an “engine of oppression” or defeat statutory rights . Both Parties are commercial entities with equal bargaining power, and this clause is voluntarily agreed upon.

11.1 Entire Agreement: This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous negotiations, representations, or agreements, whether written or oral. No representation, promise, or condition not expressly set forth herein shall be binding on either Party.

11.2 Amendments: No amendment, modification, or waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by both Parties. The failure of either Party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of such provision or of the right to enforce such provision at any later time.

11.3 Severability: If any provision of this Agreement is held by a court of competent jurisdiction to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect, and the invalid provision shall be deemed modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable while preserving the Parties’ intent.

11.4 Assignment: Neither Party may assign this Agreement or any rights or obligations hereunder, whether by operation of law or otherwise, without the prior written consent of the other Party.
