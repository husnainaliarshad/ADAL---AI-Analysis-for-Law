AND WHEREAS the Principal desires to appoint the Attorney to manage, administer, and deal with all or any of the said properties, assets, and affairs on his/her behalf due to the Principal’s residence abroad / physical incapacity / other just and sufficient reasons;

AND WHEREAS the Attorney has agreed to act as such and to discharge the duties and responsibilities entrusted hereunder;

NOW THEREFORE, the Principal hereby confers upon the Attorney the following powers, rights, and authorities:

POWERS GRANTED TO THE ATTORNEY

The Attorney shall have full, absolute, and unrestricted power and authority to do and perform all or any of the following acts, deeds, and things in the name, place, and stead of the Principal, as if the Principal were personally present and acting:

  1. Power to Manage, Sell, Transfer, and Convey Immovable Property: To ask for, demand, sue for, recover, and receive all sums of money, rents, profits, and claims due or belonging to the Principal. To sell, exchange, gift, mortgage, lease, assign, transfer, or otherwise convey any immovable property (including land, houses, shops, flats, agricultural land, commercial plots, and industrial units) belonging to the Principal, at such price, on such terms, and to such person(s) as the Attorney deems fit, and to execute, sign, and deliver all deeds, sale agreements, sale deeds, transfer instruments, possession papers, and other documents necessary for effectuating such transfer. Minimum Sale Price (if any fixed by Principal): PKR ____________________ (_______________________________ only). [Citation: 2, 4, 8]
  2. Power to Lease, Rent, or Let Out Property: To let out, lease, rent, or license any immovable property belonging to the Principal on such terms and conditions, for such duration, and at such rent as the Attorney deems fit, to execute lease deeds, rent agreements, and tenancy documents, to receive security deposits and monthly rents, to issue rent receipts, and to evict tenants or take legal action for recovery of rent or vacant possession. [Citation: 2, 10]
  3. Power to Manage Bank Accounts and Financial Transactions: To open, operate, maintain, or close any savings, current, or fixed deposit account with any bank, financial institution, or post office in the name of the Principal; to deposit and withdraw money, to endorse, sign, and encash cheques, demand drafts, pay orders, and bills of exchange; to apply for and obtain loans, advances, or credit facilities; to make investments in shares, securities, bonds, mutual funds, or government saving schemes; and to receive dividends, profits, or returns on such investments. Sale proceeds from property transactions MUST be deposited directly into the Principal’s designated bank account bearing the following details: Account Title: ______________________, Bank & Branch: ______________________, Account No.: ______________________, IBAN: ______________________. [Citation: 4, 10]
  4. Power to Appear Before the Sub-Registrar & Registration Authorities: To appear personally or through a representative before the Sub-Registrar, Joint Registrar, Assistant Registrar, or any other registration authority having jurisdiction over the property; to present, admit execution, and get registered any sale deed, gift deed, mortgage deed, lease deed, power of attorney, or any other document required to be registered under the Registration Act, 1908; to sign the register of registrations, to affix thumb impressions, and to do all acts necessary for the valid registration of documents. [Citation: 1, 8]
  5. Power to Appear Before Utility Departments (Electricity, Gas, Water, PTCL): To appear before the offices of LESCO, GEPCO, MEPCO, PESCO, HESCO, SEPCO, SNGPL, SSGC, WASA, local water and sanitation authorities, PTCL, and internet service providers; to apply for new utility connections, to transfer existing connections into the name of the Principal or purchaser/tenant, to pay bills, to dispute excessive billing, to apply for load changes or meter testing, and to sign all applications, affidavits, and indemnity bonds required by such departments. [Citation: 1]
  6. Power to Represent in Courts, Tribunals, and Government Offices: To appear, represent, and plead before any Civil Court, High Court, Supreme Court, Rent Controller, Revenue Court, Board of Revenue, Deputy Commissioner, District Coordination Officer, Excise and Taxation Office, Federal Board of Revenue (FBR), and any other judicial, quasi-judicial, or administrative authority; to file, sign, verify, and present plaints, written statements, applications, appeals, revisions, or any other legal pleadings; to appoint and instruct advocates (Vakils) on behalf of the Principal; and to receive notices, summons, and orders. [Citation: 1, 2]
  7. Power to Sign Contracts, Agreements, and Affidavits: To sign, execute, and deliver any contracts, agreements, memoranda of understanding, deeds, bonds, guarantees, indemnities, undertakings, affidavits, declarations, or any other legal documents as may be required for the management of the Principal’s affairs. [Citation: 2, 10]
  8. Power to Collect Rents, Receive Payments, and Issue Receipts: To demand, receive, and give valid receipts for all rents, profits, interest, dividends, sale proceeds, insurance claims, compensation, refunds, and any other monies due or payable to the Principal from any source whatsoever. The receipt of the Attorney shall be a full, final, and binding discharge for the party making such payment. [Citation: 2]
  9. Power to Appoint Sub-Attorneys / Associates: To appoint, engage, and instruct one or more sub-attorneys, associates, or representatives to assist in the discharge of duties hereunder, including but not limited to engaging lawyers, property dealers, surveyors, valuers, architects, or contractors. The Attorney shall remain primarily responsible for all acts of such sub-attorneys. [Citation: 1]
  10. Power to Incur Expenditure and Reimburse Expenses: To incur all necessary and reasonable expenses for the management, maintenance, repair, improvement, and protection of the Principal’s properties and affairs, including payment of taxes, utility bills, maintenance charges, insurance premiums, legal fees, and registration fees, and to reimburse himself/herself from the Principal’s funds or properties for all such expenses and for the Attorney’s lawful fees and remuneration as agreed separately or as is customary. [Citation: 2]

ACCOUNTING & REPORTING OBLIGATION

The Attorney shall keep true and proper accounts of all receipts, payments, and transactions made under this Power of Attorney. The Attorney shall, upon reasonable request by the Principal, provide written progress reports, copies of all executed documents (including sale agreements, registered sale deeds, and lease deeds), and proof of deposit of all sale proceeds and other receipts into the Principal’s designated bank account. [Citation: 4]

INDEMNITY & RATIFICATION

The Principal hereby agrees to ratify, confirm, and hold harmless the Attorney for all lawful acts, deeds, and things done by the Attorney in good faith and in pursuance of the powers granted hereunder. The Attorney shall not be liable for any loss, damage, or depreciation in value of the Principal’s properties or assets arising from market conditions or from any act done in good faith without gross negligence or willful misconduct. [Citation: 4]

REVOCATION

This General Power of Attorney shall remain in full force and effect until expressly revoked by the Principal by a written notice of revocation sent to the Attorney via registered post/courier/email, and such revocation is also published in at least two widely circulated newspapers (one Urdu, one English) and registered with the relevant Sub-Registrar’s office where this GPA was registered. The authority granted hereunder shall automatically terminate upon the death of the Principal (unless this is expressed as a Durable Power of Attorney with specific survivorship language, which is NOT the case here). [Citation: 4]

IMPORTANT LEGAL NOTES FOR EXECUTION (2026)

  • Stamp Duty (2026 rates): This GPA must be executed on non-judicial e-stamp paper of appropriate value. As of January 2026, the Power of Attorney e-stamp paper fee is PKR 1,800 [citation:3][citation:5]. For property sale matters, some jurisdictions require stamp paper of PKR 5,000 [citation:9]. Verify with local Sub-Registrar before execution.
  • Registration (Mandatory for Property): Under Section 17 of the Registration Act, 1908, if this GPA confers power to sell, transfer, or otherwise deal with immovable property, it MUST be registered with the concerned Sub-Registrar’s office. An unregistered GPA is not valid for property sale in Pakistani courts [citation:1][citation:2][citation:9].
  • For Overseas Pakistanis: If executed abroad, the GPA must be attested by the Pakistani Embassy/Consulate in that country, and then registered with the Sub-Registrar in Pakistan. NADRA online biometric verification and video interview may also be required [citation:6][citation:9].
  • Mobile SIM Requirement (2026): It is now mandatory to have a mobile SIM registered in your name to obtain any e-stamp paper [citation:5].
