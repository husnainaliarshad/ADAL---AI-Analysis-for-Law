IMPORTANT LEGAL NOTES FOR THE RECIPIENT (2026)

  • Under Section 80 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, a legal notice is mandatory before filing a civil suit against the Government or a public officer [citation: 5, 7]. In other civil matters, while not always mandatory, serving a legal notice is strongly advised to establish good faith and to provide the other party an opportunity to settle [citation: 2, 5, 8].
  • The 15-day period for compliance is considered reasonable under Pakistani law and is specifically required in consumer complaints under the Consumer Protection laws [citation: 4]. In general civil matters, courts have upheld 15-day notice periods as sufficient [citation: 2, 8, 9].
  • Failure to respond to a legal notice or to comply with its demands may result in adverse costs orders against the non-complying party, including payment of the other party’s legal fees [citation: 5].
  • This notice should be preserved by the Recipient as it may be produced as evidence in court proceedings to prove that the Recipient had actual knowledge of the claim and was given an opportunity to resolve the matter [citation: 5].
  • For disputes involving consumer complaints, a 15-day legal notice is a mandatory pre-requisite before filing a claim before the Consumer Court [citation: 4].
  • For contractual disputes, courts have held that a legal notice establishes the cause of action and provides a clear timeline of when the breach occurred [citation: 3, 5].
