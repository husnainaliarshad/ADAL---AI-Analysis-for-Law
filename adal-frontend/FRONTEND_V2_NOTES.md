# ADAL Frontend V2 Notes

This file captures product, UX, content, and visual direction for the new frontend. It is intended to be expanded incrementally as the v2 direction becomes clearer.

Reference direction currently aligns with [reference files/adal_landing.html](D:/FYP/Adal-FYP/adal-frontend/reference files/adal_landing.html).

## 1. Current Direction

- Product name: `ADAL`
- Full name: `AI-Driven Analysis for Law`
- Overall positioning: modern legal-tech product with a strong AI-native identity
- Desired feel: premium, intelligent, serious, polished, credible
- Best-fit tone: professional and confident, not overly flashy and not generic startup UI

## 2. Landing Page

### Purpose

The landing page should introduce ADAL clearly, establish trust quickly, and move the user toward sign-in through a strong first-screen experience.

### Page Structure

The landing page should be structured as:

1. Hero section
2. About section
3. Services section
4. Team section
5. Footer: recommended, minimal and clean

## 3. Hero Section

### Layout

- Full-screen section
- Main content centered both vertically and horizontally
- Strong visual focus on the project name

### Content

- Primary title: `ADAL`
- Secondary title: `AI-Driven Analysis for Law`
- Supporting copy: two professional sentences that explain the problem and position ADAL as the solution
- Primary CTA button: `Get Started`
- CTA destination: login page

### Hero Copy Direction

The copy should:

- speak to the difficulty of legal document analysis and research
- position ADAL as a faster, smarter, AI-assisted workflow
- sound professional and credible
- include a strong closing phrase or catch line

### Suggested Hero Copy

Option A:

> Legal analysis should not be slowed down by fragmented research, repetitive review, and manual document handling. ADAL brings AI-driven precision to legal workflows so you can move from documents to insight with clarity and speed.

Option B:

> Modern legal work demands more than manual review and scattered tools. ADAL helps transform dense legal material into structured insight, faster decisions, and a more intelligent workflow.

Suggested catch phrase:

> From legal text to legal intelligence.

## 4. About Section

### Purpose

This section should read like a short, polished blog-style narrative that explains what ADAL is trying to achieve.

### Content Direction

- Explain the vision behind the product
- Explain the mission in practical terms
- Emphasize legal efficiency, accessibility, and AI-assisted analysis
- Keep the tone thoughtful and credible rather than overly promotional

### Writing Style

- More editorial than marketing-heavy
- Strong hierarchy with a section title, short intro, and readable paragraph blocks
- Should feel like a serious product story

## 5. Services Section

### Purpose

This section should clearly show what ADAL offers.

### Services to Represent

The exact final list can evolve, but the landing page should be prepared to represent services such as:

- document upload and processing
- AI-powered document analysis
- legal summarization
- citation extraction
- claim extraction
- drafting assistance
- conversational legal assistance/chat
- structured workflow support for legal research

### Presentation Direction

- Use a clear section title and short intro
- Present services as a grid or stacked cards
- Each service should have a concise title and a short one- or two-line explanation

## 6. Team Section

### Purpose

This section should humanize the project and make it feel credible as a serious capstone/FYP product.

### Structure

- Section title
- A few lines introducing the team
- Three cards displayed side by side

### Team Card Design

Each card should include:

- circular image holder for member profile picture
- member name
- member title or role

### Tone

- Clean and respectable
- Not overly playful
- Should match the premium legal-tech direction of the rest of the page

## 7. Footer Recommendation

Footer is recommended.

### Why It Should Exist

- It gives the landing page a complete and intentional ending
- It improves credibility
- It gives a clean place for support links and project identity

### Suggested Footer Scope

Keep it minimal:

- ADAL name
- short one-line product descriptor
- links such as `About`, `Services`, `Team`, `Login`
- optional copyright / FYP attribution

## 8. Visual Direction

### Color Scheme

Theme name: `Obsidian & Violet`

- Obsidian: `#1A1814`
- Surface: `#2C2A26`
- Violet: `#7F77DD`
- Lavender: `#AFA9EC`
- Fog: `#EEEDFE`

### Design Intent

- AI-native prestige
- Deep intelligence
- Modern LLM-era product feel
- Strong FYP showcase presence
- Modern legal-tech positioning

### Best Use of Palette

- Obsidian as the main background
- Surface for cards, panels, and secondary sections
- Violet as the primary accent and CTA color
- Lavender for softer highlights and secondary accents
- Fog for typography and high-contrast content

## 9. Reference Style Cues

The design direction should be consistent with the current reference concept:

- dark premium background
- elegant type hierarchy
- restrained but visible glow/light effects
- strong central hero composition
- legal-tech credibility with AI-product aesthetics

Reference note included by user:

- `Bhutto v. State — PLD 1978`
- `Supreme Court · Constitutional`
- `Verified`
- `AI summary ready`
- `42 pages condensed`
- `96.4%`

These references suggest the landing page may benefit from subtle legal case metadata motifs or AI-analysis badges, as long as they do not clutter the main hero.

## 10. Open Notes

- The landing page is intended to be content-rich, not just a hero and CTA.
- The visual style should feel intentionally designed, premium, and modern.
- The footer is not mandatory, but current recommendation is to include a minimal footer.
- This is the first captured v2 idea set and should act as the basis for future refinement.

## 11. Login and Registration Page

Reference direction for this section aligns with [reference files/adal_login.html](D:/FYP/Adal-FYP/adal-frontend/reference files/adal_login.html).

### Purpose

The login page should feel like an extension of the landing page rather than a disconnected utility screen. It should preserve the premium ADAL identity while making sign-in and registration feel clear, focused, and trustworthy.

### Overall Layout

- Full-screen split layout
- Two-column composition on desktop
- Left panel for brand narrative and product credibility
- Right panel for authentication actions
- Clean, immersive, high-trust interface

### Left Panel Direction

The left side should act as a brand and product-intelligence panel rather than just decoration.

#### Left Panel Content

- ADAL wordmark at the top
- Full product name in a subtle supporting line
- Strong editorial-style headline
- One short paragraph explaining the practical value of ADAL
- Feature list showing key product capabilities
- Institutional/FYP attribution at the bottom

#### Headline Direction

Reference headline tone:

> The law is complex. Your tools should not be.

This headline direction works well because it is sharp, credible, and product-focused without sounding overly promotional.

#### Supporting Paragraph Direction

The supporting paragraph should communicate that ADAL helps legal professionals in Pakistan work faster and more intelligently through AI-supported workflows.

#### Feature List Direction

The feature list should present product capabilities in a concise, trust-building way. Based on the reference, the login page should be ready to highlight features such as:

- OCR-powered document ingestion in English and Urdu
- automatic citation verification with confidence scoring
- RAG-based precedent retrieval
- AI summarization
- AI drafting assistance with case context
- semantic search across Pakistani case law

#### Left Panel Tone

- Intelligent
- institutional
- modern legal-tech
- restrained, not loud

### Right Panel Direction

The right panel should be simple, calm, and highly usable. It should focus on account access without visual clutter.

#### Right Panel Structure

- back-to-home link in the upper-left area of the form side
- authentication form container centered vertically
- mode toggle at the top for `Sign In` and `Register`
- dynamic heading and supporting subtext
- active form below

### Authentication Mode Toggle

The login screen should support both sign-in and registration in the same overall shell.

#### Toggle Behavior

- Two-tab segmented control
- `Sign In` and `Register` as the two options
- Active state highlighted with violet accent
- Switching modes updates:
  - the active form
  - the main heading
  - the supporting subtext

This should feel smooth and modern, more like a product control than a default tab widget.

### Sign-In Form

#### Content

- Email address field
- Password field
- Password visibility toggle
- `Forgot password?` link
- Primary submit button
- Prompt below for switching to registration

#### Heading Direction

- Main heading: `Welcome back.`
- Supporting text: sign in to continue into ADAL

#### UX Direction

- Minimal and focused
- No unnecessary distractions
- Strong visual hierarchy from heading to fields to submit

### Registration Form

#### Content

- First name
- Last name
- Email address
- Role selector
- Password
- Confirm password
- Password visibility toggles
- Password strength indicator
- Primary submit button
- Terms and privacy note
- Prompt below for switching back to sign-in

#### Role Options

The reference suggests these user-role choices:

- Lawyer / Advocate
- Law Student
- Legal Researcher
- Other

These may be adjusted later depending on actual product scope, but the page should be designed to accommodate role-based onboarding.

#### Heading Direction

- Main heading: `Create your account.`
- Supporting text: join ADAL and modernize the legal workflow

### Form Styling Direction

#### Inputs

- Dark surface inputs
- Thin violet-tinted borders
- Compact but elegant spacing
- Soft focus state with stronger violet emphasis
- Clean labels in small uppercase format

#### Buttons

- Primary CTA in violet
- Uppercase label styling
- Slight hover lift
- Subtle motion, not aggressive animation

#### Micro-Interactions

- Password visibility toggle
- animated form-panel switching
- password strength feedback
- subtle hover states for links and buttons

### Back-to-Home Link

This should remain part of the design.

#### Reason

- It preserves continuity with the landing page
- It makes the authentication route feel intentionally connected to the marketing entry point
- It improves navigation without adding clutter

### Footer / Attribution on Login Screen

The reference includes FYP/institutional attribution in the left panel footer. This is a good fit and should be preserved in some form.

#### Suggested Content

- Department or institutional line
- university name
- location and year

This helps the page feel grounded and purposeful, especially for FYP presentation.

### Visual Direction for Login Page

The login page should remain within the same `Obsidian & Violet` system as the landing page.

#### Important Visual Cues from Reference

- dark split-screen composition
- premium serif-plus-sans pairing
- subtle grid texture
- restrained glow effect
- polished panel borders
- calm, high-contrast form surfaces

### Login Page Intent Summary

The login/register page should feel like:

- a premium product entry point
- an intelligent legal-tech system
- a serious platform for professional use
- visually cohesive with the landing page

It should not feel like a generic student project login form or a default dashboard auth screen.
