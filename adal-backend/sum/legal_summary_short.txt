The Supreme Court of Pakistan dismissed the appeals of M/s Sazco (Pvt.) Ltd., upholding the decisions of the Banking Court and Peshawar High Court in favour of Askari Commercial Bank Limited. The critical issue was whether the bank had wrongfully accepted allegedly forged or discrepant bills of lading under irrevocable letters of credit governed by UCP 500. The Court reaffirmed the autonomy and strict compliance principles of documentary credits, holding that Sazco failed to prove fraud or non-conformity of documents, thus entitling the bank to payment.
